Gari Clifford, D.Phil.


Associate Professor, Department of Biomedical Informatics @ Emory University School of Medicine
Associate Professor, Department of Biomedical Engineering @ Georgia Institute of Technology
Acting Director, Affordable Health Technologies @ The George Institute for Global Health
Director, Centre for Affordable Healthcare Technology @ Kellogg College
Honorary Professor, The Sleep & Circadian Neuroscience Institute @ University of Oxford
James Martin Fellow, Oxford Martin School
Dr. Clifford is trained as a PhD in Neural Networks and Biomedical Engineering from the University of Oxford. He received postdoctoral research training from MIT where he later became a Principal Research Scientist, managing the development of the world's largest open access critical care database (MIMIC II). Since 2009, he has served as Associate Professor and Fellow of Kellogg College at the University of Oxford in the Department of Engineering Science, led the Intelligent Patient Monitoring Group, and served as Director of the Centre for Doctoral Training in Healthcare Innovation at the Institute of Biomedical Engineering (IBME). Dr. Clifford has an international reputation in critical care data analysis and the application of signal processing and machine learning to medicine. Dr. Clifford helped found the Sleep, Circadian Rhythm and Neuroscience Institute (SCNi) at the University of Oxford, where he is an Honorary Professor and leads one of its five themes.
