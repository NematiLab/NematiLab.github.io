Myfanwy Hopkins, Ph.D.

Research Informatics Analyst
Biomedical Informatics at Emory University

Dr. Hopkins serves as a healthcare database engineer, facilitating the transition of data to knowledge. She works with the four V's of big healthcare data: volume, velocity, variety and veracity. The goal is not simply to create new visualizations of physiological data, but to build scaffolding for data processing to facilitate investigation, discovery and implantation.

David Springer

D.Phil. candidate
Institute of Biomedical Engineering, University of Oxford

David is developing a low-cost mobile phone-based stethoscope for the automatic detection of rheumatic heart disease as part of his PhD at Oxford. He is part of the Intelligent Patient Monitoring group in the Institute of Biomedical Engineering, where he is using machine learning and signal processing techniques to classify the signal quality of heart sounds, detect the positions of the salient sounds and classify the presence of patholigical murmurs in phonocardiograms. He holds a Bsc. (hons) from the University of Cape Town and the class medal for the MSc. in Biomedical Engineering at the University of Oxford. He has worked on other mobile health-based projects, including a low-cost blood pressure device and the largest SMS-text message medication adherence support trial to date.

Joachim Behar

D.Phil. candidate
Institute of Biomedical Engineering, University of Oxford

Joachim is currently finishing his PhD in Signal Processing and Machine Learning within the Intelligent Patient Monitoring Group (IPMGroup) at the University of Oxford, Department of Engineering Science where he is developing machine learning and Bayesian filtering approaches for non-invasive fetal electrocardiogram extraction from abdominal sensors. Joachim holds a MEng from l'Ecole Nationale Superieure des Mines de Saint-Etienne in France in General Engineering and an MSc in Biomedical Engineering from Oxford. He is co-founder of the Oxford Engineering World Health Chapter (now know as Oxcaht), has been involved in numerous engineering projects for the developing world including the MIT Sana telemedicine project, the Oxford Smart Handpumps and SleepAp projects. Joachim has worked as a DSP & ML consultant for local startups and is prize winner of the MIT-Phyionet Challenge 2013 & 2014 competitions.
